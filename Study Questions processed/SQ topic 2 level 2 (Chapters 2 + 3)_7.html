The chemical behavior of atoms is mostly dependent on their valence electrons, which are the electrons in their outermost, or valence, electron shell. Depending on the number of electrons the atoms of a particular element have, the electrons may be sufficient to fill only the 1st electron shell, leaving the 1st shell as the outermost shell. Atoms of elements with more electrons may have sufficient electrons to fill the first electron shell and to partially or fully fill the 2nd shell, but not to place any electrons in the 3rd electron shell. In that case, the 2nd
shell would be the outermost shell that has electrons and would thus be the valence shell.

Bases cause the hydrogen ion concentration of a solution to DECREASE. Remember that, because pH is the NEGATIVE log of the hydrogen ion concentration, when the hydrogen ion concentration of a solution decreases, the pH of that solution increases. So, bases cause the pH of a solution to INCREASE, rather than decrease.
 
Bases cause the concentration of H+ ions to decrease by one of two ways. Some bases reduce H+ concentrations by dissociating to form hydroxide (OH-) anions. The hydroxide ions from the bases then react with H+ in the water to form water molecules, thereby reducing H+ concentrations. Alternatively, some bases reduce H+ concentrations by accepting (binding with) hydrogen ions.
 
So, because the question asks which statement about bases is FALSE, the correct answer to the question is that bases cause the pH to decrease, since bases actually cause the pH to increase.
